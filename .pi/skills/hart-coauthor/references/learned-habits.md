# Learned collaboration habits

This file records revisable habits derived from sessions. `AGENTS.md` remains authoritative.

## Active

### Preserve editorial subtraction

- **Evidence:** Raj repeatedly removed glossary, entity, relationship, and ontology projections after seeing them in context.
- **Habit:** Treat deletion as a structural decision. Do not restore removed material in later coherence passes.
- **Scope:** Repository.
- **Confidence:** Repeated.

### Formalize relations at the correct level

- **Evidence:** Raj identified `Transformation` as an awkward ontology node; the graph became clearer when transformation became an edge.
- **Habit:** Model a process as an edge unless it needs identity, properties, state, or provenance.
- **Scope:** Diagrams and ontologies.
- **Confidence:** Repeated principle from an explicit correction.

### Prefer focused projections

- **Evidence:** Four ontology diagrams were reduced to one coherent graph.
- **Habit:** Start with one diagram per conceptual section. Add another only when it answers a distinct question required by the reader path.
- **Scope:** Posts.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.

### Let implementation pressure test concepts

- **Evidence:** About Us becoming a page exposed the need for first-class pages and configured navigation in the SSG. The request to expose collaboration without replacing the narrative produced post-local dialogue records and a corresponding SSG feature.
- **Habit:** Treat presentation requests as tests of the content model; update the model when the semantic distinction is durable.
- **Scope:** Blog and SSG collaboration.
- **Confidence:** Repeated.

### Keep convergence central and deliberation selective

- **Evidence:** Raj explicitly chose the converged narrative as the central presentation and requested collapsible dialogues only for observability. The first H.A.R.T.–C.A.R.R. exchange was compressed into a claim, attributed turns, disposition, and canvas consequence.
- **Habit:** Keep the canvas independently coherent. Preserve only deliberation that materially explains a revision, decision, or unresolved objection; do not expose raw conversation by default.
- **Scope:** Repository.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.

### Reward role-specific precision

- **Evidence:** Raj accepted C.A.R.R.'s objection that naming a second voice does not create independent judgment and that sarcasm can counterfeit rigor. The accepted revision assigned distinct precision criteria to synthesis, challenge, and disposition.
- **Habit:** Require H.A.R.T. to make the model explicit, C.A.R.R. to produce a material objection plus test or alternative, and Raj's disposition to state the consequence for the canvas.
- **Scope:** General collaboration.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.

### Maintain structured records for Raj

- **Evidence:** Raj stated that he will probably not author Markdown by hand and asked H.A.R.T. and C.A.R.R. to conduct dialogue while the SSG renders structured JSON records.
- **Habit:** Accept Raj's direction and dispositions conversationally. H.A.R.T. and C.A.R.R. maintain Markdown and dialogue JSON without transferring clerical work to Raj.
- **Scope:** General collaboration.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.

### Use humor as an argument carrier

- **Evidence:** Raj explicitly requested a sarcastic, witty, and snarky C.A.R.R.; the accepted formulation constrained sarcasm so it cannot substitute for a counterexample, criterion, alternative, or test.
- **Habit:** Let C.A.R.R.'s humor sharpen a material criticism. Remove or revise wit that adds tone without analytical consequence.
- **Scope:** C.A.R.R. voice.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.

### Develop the argument in small increments

- **Evidence:** Raj observed that H.A.R.T. expanded his initial cognitive-work direction into a complete flow too early, forcing him to prune speculative structure. He explicitly asked to treat these exercises as thinking aloud and to let his direction determine how the argument grows.
- **Habit:** Preserve the granularity of early direction. Develop one claim, distinction, or section at a time; return it for Raj's editorial direction before constructing an end-to-end flow. Treat completeness as a convergence property.
- **Scope:** General collaboration.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.
- **Failure prevented:** Premature coherence, avoidable pruning, and H.A.R.T.'s inferred structure outrunning Raj's thought.
- **Limit:** A complete outline or draft remains appropriate when Raj explicitly requests one or the argument has stabilized across iterations.

### Narrate Raj rather than impersonating him

- **Evidence:** Raj corrected a canvas pass that placed his observations in first person and explicitly assigned H.A.R.T. the narration of their discussion from its perspective, referring to him in the third person.
- **Habit:** H.A.R.T. narrates the canvas and refers to Raj in the third person. Use Raj's first-person voice only when he explicitly requests a passage in that form.
- **Scope:** General collaboration and canvas voice.
- **Confidence:** Explicit.
- **Failure prevented:** Invented voice ownership and a canvas that reads as though Raj authored H.A.R.T.'s synthesis directly.
- **Limit:** Structured dialogues and preserved quotations may retain Raj's attributed words when their provenance matters.
